A single fake one-star review can cost a contractor thousands of dollars in lost jobs. This guide walks through exactly how to identify and remove reviews that violate Google's policies.

## What counts as a removable review?

Google only removes reviews that break its published policies — fake engagement, conflict of interest, off-topic content, spam, or prohibited content. A genuinely unhappy customer's honest review does not qualify, and trying to remove it can backfire.

## How do you report a fake review?

Open your Google Business Profile, find the review, and use the report flow to flag the specific policy it violates. Provide clear evidence the reviewer was never a customer or has a conflict of interest.

## How long does removal take?

It varies by case and Google's review queue — some come down in days, others take longer. Persistence and a well-documented policy violation improve your odds.
